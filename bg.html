British Gas – complaints

British Gas receive around 200k complaints a year via their online channels (chat, social, e-form, email).

Original British Gas complaints page

The problem

  1. British Gas receive a high volume of complaints via email and letter, which are their worst performing channels. They take the longest to resolve with lowest satisfaction scores for the customer.
  2. Web chat only accounts for 2-3% of demand, yet provides a speedier resolution and better experience for the customer.
  3. The web chat channel receives a high share of general enquiries (around 50%) which wastes agent time and creates cost.
  4. The web chat channel also deals with a high amount of customers who want to complain, but have come through to the wrong department. Again, this wastes agent time and creates cost.

I was tasked with redesigning the complaints flow to address all these problems.

Insights

Through several meetings with stakeholders we were able to gain a good understanding of the customer and business processes for a typical online complaint.

We established potential pain points, costs and the average time each channel would typically take to resolve a complaint.

To better understand what types of conversations our agents were having with customers we sampled 60 web chat transcripts from a single day in June.

We found that 46% of the chats were general enquiries.

Of the enquiries, 21% were related to a British Gas engineer visit, 14% were about HomeCare and 10% were about Direct Debits or tariffs.

Prototype and test

Using the insights gained from our stakeholders and chat transcripts we designed a new flow with three major changes.

Firstly, we designed a multi page flow which took users through a triage to help them understand what type of complaint they had.

Proposed multi page complaints flow

Secondly, we surfaced the most common types of enquiries on the startpage. We hoped this would help to divert some of the non-complaints customers away from the complaints journey.

Start page

Finally, we added a large web chat lockup on the final page which we hoped would attract more clicks and increase the web chat demand.

Web chat lockup

We tested the prototype with 5 users (1 British Gas customer, 4 non British Gas customers).

Key findings

  1. Users who had incorrectly selected their departmental option on the existing page were able to correctly select their option on the prototype.
  2. When users were asked to resolve a general enquiry they successfully navigated away from the complaints journey.
  3. All users saw and recognised the web chat option at the ends of their journeys.

Launch

View the new complaints journey – released September 2020.

At the time of writing (November 2020), the web chat demand has increased from 2-3% to around 10%.

Agents are now speaking to fewer complaint customers who’ve come through to the wrong department.

However, general enquiries are still coming through to the complaints agents, but we understand this is down to other factors outside the control and the remit of this project.
